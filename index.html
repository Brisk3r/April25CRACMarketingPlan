Executive Summary

The recent grand opening of the Clarence Regional Aquatic Centre (CRAC) presents a significant opportunity to establish this state-of-the-art facility as a premier destination for recreation, fitness, and community engagement within the Clarence Valley. This 6-Month Marketing Activation Plan provides a comprehensive strategy to build awareness, drive visitation, foster community integration, and maximise revenue generation during the critical initial operating period. The plan leverages the Centre's modern facilities, addresses the specific demographic context of the region, and outlines targeted strategies across digital marketing, community outreach, aquatic partnerships, and key booking streams. Key performance indicators (KPIs) are defined to measure success, alongside considerations for future growth opportunities. The immediate focus includes capitalising on launch momentum, clarifying and promoting key revenue streams like birthday parties and room hire, and establishing strong foundations for long-term community value and operational sustainability.

1. Introduction

1.1. Centre Overview

The Clarence Regional Aquatic Centre, located at the corner of Oliver and Turf Streets in Grafton, represents a major investment in community infrastructure for the Clarence Valley from the council. Replacing the former Grafton Olympic Pool, which closed due to structural concerns in September 2022, the new Centre officially opened in stages during early 2025, with the 50m outdoor pool, the indoor facilities and splash pad, and the official grand opening ceremony held on March 22nd, 2025.

Constructed by Hines Construction Pty Ltd, the facility boasts:

Outdoor Facilities:

  • An 8-lane, 50-metre pool suitable for regional competitions
  • An inclusive zero-depth splash pad
  • Grandstand seating with wheelchair access
  • Picnic/shelter areas

Indoor Facilities:

  • An accessible 8-lane, 25-metre lap pool (FINA standards)
  • An accessible combined program and leisure pool
  • Tiered seating
  • Modern accessible change rooms (including adult change facilities)
  • On-deck showers

General Amenities:

  • Male/female change rooms, Parents' room
  • Large multipurpose community room, Bookable community room
  • Dedicated first aid room, Admin offices
  • Scoring/storage rooms

The Centre offers Learn to Swim and Squad programs, casual swimming, and is recruiting for programs like Aqua Fitness and Elite Squads. Future enhancements include two recreational waterslides, expected by the end of 2025. The indoor pools holds historical significance, named the Grafton Cub Scout Memorial in remembrance of a local tragedy. The Centre operates extensive hours, seven days a week all year round.

1.2. Purpose and Scope of the Marketing Plan

This document outlines a strategic marketing plan for the first six months of operation following the Clarence Regional Aquatic Centre's grand opening. The purpose is to:

  • Generate widespread awareness and excitement within the Clarence Valley community.
  • Drive initial and repeat visitation across various user segments.
  • Establish strong relationships with community groups, schools, and sporting organisations.
  • Maximise utilisation of all facility components, including pools, the community room, and party bookings.
  • Build a foundation for long-term financial sustainability and community value.

The plan encompasses situational analysis, defined objectives, target audience identification, detailed marketing strategies (digital, community, partnerships, bookings), identification of further opportunities, and key performance indicators for measuring success.

2. Situational Analysis

2.1. SWOT Analysis

A SWOT analysis provides a framework for understanding the Centre's current position:

Strengths

  • New, State-of-the-Art Facility: Modern design, brand new equipment, appealing aesthetics.
  • Diverse Offerings: Caters to various needs – lap swimming (50m/25m), learn-to-swim, squads, leisure (program pool), family fun (splash pad), fitness (Aqua).
  • Year-Round Access: Heated indoor pools ensure consistent availability regardless of weather.
  • Accessibility: Designed with inclusivity in mind (accessible pools, change rooms, seating).
  • Competition Standard: FINA standard 25m pool and regional competition capable 50m pool attract higher-level use.
  • Supporting Amenities: Club room, storage, multipurpose room, picnic areas enhance user experience.
  • Community Demand: Highly anticipated project following closure of old pool and community campaigning.

Weaknesses

  • Newness Factor: Potential for initial operational challenges or "teething problems" as staff and systems settle in.
  • Undefined Offerings: Lack of clear, publicly available information on birthday party packages and community room hire specifics (capacity, pricing, booking process) hinders immediate revenue generation.
  • Location Perception: Situated on the site of the previous olympic pool, potentially causing confusion with previous maps and socials, could require targeted effort to overcome.
  • Past Delays: Initial construction delays, partly due to weather, may have impacted initial summer season momentum and therefore community perception. (has not shown effect on community feedback yet).

Opportunities

  • Health & Wellness Focus: Capitalise on growing community interest in health, fitness, and active lifestyles; position aquatic activity for rehabilitation and general wellbeing.
  • Targeted Bookings: Significant revenue potential from school carnivals, birthday parties, and community room hire once packages are defined and marketed.
  • Community Partnerships: Collaborate with schools, community groups, businesses, health providers, and Indigenous organisations for mutual benefit and increased reach.
  • Aquatic Sport Development: Become the hub for local swim clubs and potentially host regional/state events, leveraging high-standard facilities.
  • Waterslide Addition: Future installation of waterslides (by end 2025) will provide a major new attraction and marketing angle.
  • Program Expansion: Introduce new classes and programs based on community feedback and demand.

Threats

  • Economic Factors: Lower median household income in the region may make cost a barrier for some potential users; discretionary spending could be impacted by broader economic conditions.
  • Competition: Other recreational activities, sports, and entertainment options compete for residents' time and money.
  • Operational Issues: Any significant issues with water quality, staffing, or maintenance could negatively impact reputation after the shutdown.
  • Seasonality: While indoor facilities mitigate this, outdoor usage is a key feature of the facility and can significantly change with weather/seasons.

The newness of the facility is a double-edged sword; while its modern features are a major strength, it also means operational processes are still being embedded. Marketing must strongly emphasise the benefits of the new infrastructure while customer service manages expectations. Furthermore, the facility's capacity to support health and wellness goals should be highlighted, appealing to the region's demographic profile which includes an older median age and reported higher rates of certain long-term health conditions. Addressing the current lack of defined packages for birthday parties and the community room is critical; benchmarks from other centres show these are standard offerings and represent immediate revenue opportunities that are currently untapped.

The demographic data highlights a crucial duality. The broader Clarence Valley has an older median age and a significant proportion of households without children, suggesting demand for lap swimming, aqua fitness, and potentially hydrotherapy programs in the versatile program pool. However, the Centre's immediate vicinity, Grafton, has a considerably younger median age (40 vs 49) and a very high proportion of Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander residents (14.6%). This points to a strong local market for family-oriented activities like Learn to Swim, the splash pad, and birthday parties. The lower median household income across the region, particularly in Grafton, underscores the importance of communicating value and ensuring pricing structures (including family and multi-visit passes) are perceived as accessible. Marketing messages and outreach must be nuanced to appeal to these distinct segments within the overall catchment area. Specific, targeted outreach to the Grafton community, acknowledging its unique profile, will be more effective than a purely region-wide approach.

3. Marketing Objectives (First 6 Months)

Based on the situational analysis, the following SMART (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound) objectives are proposed for the initial 6-month period:

  • Continue to Set Baseline Centre Visitation: Achieve an average monthly attendance base/increase of X% (Target is difficult due to change of season and new business) by Month 6, measured via entry data.
  • Drive Program Enrolment: Secure baseline total enrolments across Learn to Swim, Squads, and Aqua Fitness programs by the end of Month 6, measured via registration system data. Achieve an average class fill rate of 50-80% for scheduled programs.
  • Grow Digital Audience & Engagement: Increase Facebook page followers by 50% and establish an Instagram by Month 6. Achieve an average monthly engagement rate of 10% - 20% across platforms, measured via platform analytics. Build an email subscriber list of various per category contacts with an average open rate of 10% - 20% by Month 6.
  • Establish Key Booking Revenue Streams: Secure school carnival bookings for the following season beginning in term 2, generate birthday party bookings, and achieve baseline hours of community room hire by Month 6, measured via booking system logs.
  • Foster Community Integration: Establish formal or informal partnerships with local schools (starting comms in term 2), 3 community groups/non-profits, and 2 local businesses by Month 6 coming into summer, tracked via internal records. (for example Harvey Norman)
  • Build Swim Club Relationships: Secure lane hire agreements with the local swim club for regular training by Month 6. (this has already started to take shape under Kia's bookings post south side’s closure)

4. Target Audiences

Based on demographics and facility offerings, key target audiences include:

  • Families with Young Children (Ages 0-12): Primary market for Learn to Swim, splash pad, leisure pool, birthday parties, and family casual swims. Strong concentration in Grafton. Need value, convenience, and fun.
  • School-Aged Children & Teenagers (Ages 5-18): Market for Learn to Swim, Squads, school carnivals, casual swimming, potentially youth events, and future waterslides. Significant numbers in local schools.
  • Adult Fitness Swimmers: Utilise the 50m and 25m lap pools. Interested in lap swimming, potentially squads or master's swimming. Seek consistent lane availability and potentially multi-visit passes.
  • Health & Wellness Seekers (All Ages, including Seniors): Interested in low-impact exercise, Aqua Fitness, rehabilitation, and general wellbeing. Includes the significant older demographic in the Valley. Accessibility features are important.
  • Local Schools (Primary & Secondary): Key market for annual swimming carnivals and potentially regular PE programs or reward days. Need reliable booking and safety compliance.
  • Community Groups & Local Businesses: Potential users of the multipurpose community room for meetings, events, or training. Businesses may be interested in corporate wellness or sponsorships (for example fitness passport). Includes seniors groups, youth services, disability providers, Indigenous organisations.
  • Local Swim Clubs & Aquatic Sporting Bodies: Market for club nights, hosting competitions and end of season ceremonies. Require specific facilities and competition-standard pools. Need engagement with Swimming NSW, Swimming North Coast and local clubs.

5. Marketing Strategies

5.1. Digital Presence & Communication

5.1.1. Social Media Strategy

Goal: Build an engaged online community, showcase the facility, promote programs/events, and drive visitation.

Platforms:

  • Facebook: Primary platform for broad reach, event promotion, sharing detailed information, community Q&A, and linking to booking systems. Leverage Clarence Valley Council's existing Facebook presence initially but establish a dedicated CRAC page for focused content.
  • Instagram (Set-up Needed): Focus on high-quality visual content – photos and short videos showcasing the facilities, activities (swimming, classes, parties), atmosphere, and user experiences (testimonials, action shots). Use Stories for behind-the-scenes glimpses and timely updates. Drive traffic to Facebook or website for details.

Content Pillars:

  • Facility Showcase & Program Spotlight
  • Event Announcements & User Generated Content
  • Health & Wellness Tips & Operational Updates

Content Mix & Schedule: Aim for 3-5 posts per week on Facebook, 2-4 posts/stories per week on Instagram. Mix formats: high-quality photos, short engaging videos (e.g., splash pad action, lap swimming), graphics for announcements, occasional Facebook Live Q&A sessions with staff (pending staff engagement). Use consistent branding (logos, colours). Respond promptly to comments and messages. Consider a small budget for boosting key posts (e.g., LTS enrolment open, party package launch).

Hashtags: Use a mix of general and specific hashtags (e.g., #ClarenceRegionalAquaticCentre #GraftonPool #SwimCRAC #MyClarenceValley #SwimLocal #LearnToSwim #FamilyFun #AquaFitness #SchoolCarnival).

A dual-platform approach is recommended. Facebook serves as the information hub, aligning with existing Council communication channels and reaching a broad demographic. Instagram's visual nature is essential for conveying the appeal of the new, modern facility, particularly features like the splash pad and indoor pools. Content must be adapted; Facebook can host detailed text and links, while Instagram requires compelling imagery and concise captions, directing users elsewhere for comprehensive information. Cross-promotion between platforms will maximise reach.

5.1.2. Email Marketing Strategy

Goal: Build a subscriber database for direct communication, promote programs and offers, and nurture leads.

List Building:

  • Website Sign-up: Prominent sign-up form on the CRAC section of the Council website or a future dedicated site. Offer an incentive (e.g., chance to win a 10-visit pass each month).
  • In-Centre Registration: Collect emails during program enrolment, pass purchase, or via QR code at reception. Ensure clear opt-in consent.
  • Event Bookings: Capture emails through online booking systems.

Content Plan:

  • Welcome Email: Introduce centre, facilities, links to timetables, pricing, promotions.
  • Monthly Newsletter: LTS dates, new classes, offers, event calendar, facility news, health tips.
  • Targeted Emails (Future): Segment list by interest for relevant content.

Platform: Utilise an Email Service Provider (e.g., Mailchimp, Campaign Monitor, Aiming for Perfect Gym) for professional templates, list management, automation, and performance tracking.

Compliance: Ensure all email collection and communication adheres to Australian Privacy Principles and anti-spam legislation (explicit opt-in required).

The existing online registration system for Learn to Swim and Squads, which already captures email addresses for waitlists, presents a prime opportunity. Integrating an explicit opt-in checkbox for marketing communications within this system will streamline list growth by leveraging an existing operational process, capturing users already engaged with specific programs.

5.1.3. Google Business Profile (GBP) Integration

Goal: Maximise local visibility in Google Search and Maps, provide essential information easily, build trust through reviews and photos, and drive direct engagement (calls, website visits, directions).

Setup & Verification: Claim/create and verify GBP listing. Address duplicates. Remove old pool listing.

Optimisation Best Practices (Summary): Complete info (NAP, hours, website), NAP consistency, accurate categories, detailed attributes (accessibility, amenities, offerings), high-quality photos/videos, compelling description, list all services, regular Google Posts, monitor Q&A, actively manage reviews, consider messaging.

Impact: A well-optimised GBP is crucial for local search rankings, driven by relevance, distance, and prominence.

5.3. Cultivating Aquatic Partnerships: Clubs & Governing Bodies

Goal: Establish CRAC as the preferred venue for local swim clubs and position it to host sanctioned events by engaging effectively with official swimming bodies.

Engaging Local Swim Clubs & Hosting Events:

  • Identify & Connect: Research and contact existing clubs.
  • Promote Club Facilities: Market dedicated club room, storage, pools.
  • Develop Lane Hire Agreements: Clear, competitive pricing.
  • Facilitate Club Events: Solicit bookings for race nights, meets.
  • Support Club Development: Collaborate on pathways from LTS/Squads.

Collaboration with Official Swimming Bodies (Swimming NSW):

  • Understand Affiliation: Be knowledgeable on process.
  • Meet Sanctioning Awareness: Understand requirements.
  • Build Governing Body Relationships: Contact Area representative.
  • Promote Development Pathways: Highlight grassroots to elite.

The Centre's possession of a FINA-standard 25m indoor pool and a 50m outdoor pool designed for regional level competitions represents a significant competitive advantage. These technical specifications meet the requirements for serious training and sanctioned competition. This capability should be explicitly marketed to swim clubs and Swimming NSW, positioning CRAC not merely as a community pool, but as a high-calibre aquatic sports venue capable of hosting significant events, thereby enhancing its regional profile and justifying appropriate facility hire fees.

  • Package Development: Create "School Carnival Package" brochure/web page detailing facilities, safety, booking, pricing, and optional add-ons.
  • Outreach Strategy: Direct mail/email campaign in Term 3/4 for following year bookings, follow up in Term 1. Offer site inspections.

  • Target Audience: Families with children aged approx. 4-12 years.
  • Promotional Tactics: Dedicated webpage/flyer, social media launch, in-centre posters, targeted emails, local parent website listings.

Marketing the Community Room:

Urgent Action - Define Offerings: Immediately finalise and publish details for the "Large multipurpose room".
